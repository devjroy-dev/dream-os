## The ruling, clauses 1–7

1. **Destination: Meta WhatsApp Cloud API, DIRECT, with TDW registered as the Tech Provider.** TDW owns its WABAs and Business Portfolio outright. Rationale of record: the per-number BSP tax is the one cost that scales linearly with vendor count; direct removes it entirely, gives the rawest API + webhooks for a custom AI backend, and yields the least lock-in. For a company whose product IS the AI back-office, owning the transport layer is the strategically correct seat. The accepted price is operational: Embedded Signup onboarding, own webhook infra, quality monitoring, thin Meta-direct support.
2. **MSG91: demoted to a footnote.** It was the sanctioned bridge (near pass-through ₹500/number, INR + GST invoices, developer-first API, portable migration) contingent on a Meta-verification stall — and clause 7 retires that contingency. It remains named here only as the known best India-native BSP should circumstances ever change.
3. **Twilio: fallback only, never the front-runner** for this profile — markup on both inbound and outbound (double exposure exactly where an inbound-heavy service product's volume sits), USD billing (FX drag, reverse-charge GST rather than a clean Indian tax invoice), support not India-timezone-first. Choose it only if SMS fallback / global expansion ever dominates.
4. **Excluded: AiSensy, WATI, Interakt (campaign-and-inbox SaaS — paying for a chatbot UI this product explicitly does not want) and 360dialog (excellent engineering, per-number pricing disqualifying at per-vendor scale).**
5. **The template constraint, named early as an 05 work item:** under the official API, every BUSINESS-INITIATED message must live in an approved template. The estate's existing outbound machinery — the bride nudges (`brideNudge.js` / `brideCron.js`) and any future Donna-initiated sends — must be template-shaped at 05's migration. **First-week 05 item (enabled by clause 7): file the outbound template shapes with Meta EARLY, so approval latency never sits on 05's critical path.**
6. **Entry = direct from day 1.** The bridge bought only hand-holding; the direct path's real demands (webhook re-point — the estate already runs WA webhooks; template submissions; Embedded Signup as a build item) are integration work this team does as its method. The A-5 WA mode words are unaffected in doctrine and re-sited in mechanism at the swap: they ship now on the current engines at the pre-engine seam (`src/index.js`, the vendor_self lane), and carry over to the new webhook path when 05 swaps the transport — expected, not rework.
7. **TDW's Meta WABA is ALREADY GRANTED** (founder, 2026-07-18): Business verification and the WhatsApp Business Account exist today. The day-1-direct entry's only real friction — bureaucratic patience at verification — is retired before 05 opens. 05's transport work is purely build.

## The number architecture (the two lanes, ruled apart)

- **Vendor ↔ assistant lane (today's product): SHARED numbers, sender-resolved — retained.** The owner messages the TDW line; the sender identifies the vendor; one number serves the fleet indefinitely. Nothing changes here at the swap except the transport beneath it.
- **Client-facing lane (P2/P3 era): ONE DEDICATED NUMBER PER VENDOR — chartered.** Three stacking reasons of record: identity (a bride messaging "Rahul Photography" must see that business answering, never a generic shared line); quality isolation (WhatsApp scores and bans PER NUMBER — one vendor's aggressive sends must not take down the fleet; per-vendor numbers cap the blast radius); template sender identity (business-initiated messages go out under the number's registered business). The vendor's number may be freshly provisioned or their existing BUSINESS line migrated onto the API — never their personal number (an API number cannot simultaneously run the consumer WhatsApp app). Under direct Cloud API the per-number PLATFORM tax is zero; costs are number provisioning + Meta's per-conversation fees — which is precisely why clause 1's BSP-tax removal matters at fleet scale.
- **Risk posture accepted with clause 1:** sole responsibility for quality-rating and ban management on AI-generated messaging. Standing mitigants, all already load-bearing in the design: the traffic's center of gravity is user-initiated (the safest category); per-vendor numbers isolate blast radius; every proactive message rides an approved utility template with marketing kept minimal.
